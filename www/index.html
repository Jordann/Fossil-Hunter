Ammonites

Ammonites

Pronounced – Am-mon-nite
Meaning of name – The shell resembles the coiled ram’s horn (a ram was the symbol for the Egyptian god Ammon)
Group – Cephalopods, Mollusc
Age – Lower Jurassic, around 195 million years old


Ammonites are a well known fossil and easily recognised by their coiled shell. They first appeared around 400 million years ago and became a very successful group of animals. They died out around the same time as the dinosaurs about 65 million years ago. Ammonites were free swimming creatures related to squid and octopuses. Like these modern relatives they would have been predators, catching prey with their long tentacles. Their shell was divided up into chambers filled with liquid and gas, which kept them buoyant in the water. They can be preserved in a number of different ways.

The Fossil Code

Home

The Visitors Fossil Collecting Code is shown below, these are some of the best tips for fossil hunting safely.

The best place to find fossils

The best place to find fossils is on the beach, look for fossils trapped between boulders and among pebbles on the beach.

Stay away from the cliffs

Stay away from the cliffs, as rockfalls can happen at any time. Please do not hammer or dig in the cliffs.

Keep away from the cliff edge

Clifs can crumble at any time, always avoid staning near the cliffs edge and pay attention to all warning signs.

Always collect on a falling tide

Beware of large waves, especially during rough weather.

Mudflows are dangerous

Stay well away or you could lose more than your wellies!

Always tell others your plans

Make sure you tell someone, where you are going and how long you are going to be.

Download the full code

Finally, good luck and enjoy your fossil collecting!
